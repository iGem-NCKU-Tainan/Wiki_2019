Overview

We aim to develop mathematical models that act as a bridge between the theoretical and the physical realisation of our biological work, to optimise our experiments and to understand our results.

We demonstrated that our bacteriocin can specifically reduce Clostridium difficile population in human intestines. However, it is deemed difficult to purify bacteriocin after production due to technical limitations. That is why we modelled bacteriocin's effectiveness in reducing Clostridium difficile population in our experiments.

To complement our p-Cresol biosensing system, we modelled the relationships between p-Cresol concentration, bacteria growth and fluorescent protein expression, then used the model to estimate the number of E. coli needed to measure blood p-Cresol level effectively.
